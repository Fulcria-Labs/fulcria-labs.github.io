Every divot tells a story. Most golfers ignore this free feedback, but divot patterns reveal exactly what's happening at impact.

Learn to read your divots, and you can diagnose swing problems without any technology.

## Divot Direction

After hitting an iron, look at where your divot points:

### Pointing left of target (for right-handed golfer):

Outside-in swing path. This produces pulls, pull-slices, and weak fades. Your club is cutting across the ball.

### Pointing right of target:

Inside-out swing path. This produces pushes, push-draws, and draw/hooks. Your club is traveling toward right field through impact.

### Pointing at target:

Square path. Combined with a square face, this produces straight shots. The goal for most golfers.

## Divot Depth

### Deep divots:

Steep angle of attack. Common causes: over-the-top move, poor weight shift, ball position too far back.

### Shallow or no divot:

Flat angle of attack. Could indicate good compression (if ball flight is solid) or thin contact (if shots are thin). Look at ball flight to interpret.

### Divot behind ball (fat shot):

Club bottoming out too early. Often from improper weight shift or trying to help the ball up.

## Divot Location

### Divot starts at the ball or after:

Correct contact. The club reaches its low point at or just after the ball, compressing it into the turf.

### Divot starts behind ball:

Fat contact. The club is hitting ground before ball, losing energy and distance.

## Using Divots for Diagnosis

| Problem | Divot Pattern | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| Slice + deep divots | Points left, deep | Over-the-top with steep angle | Work on shallowing and inside path |
| Hooks + shallow divots | Points right, shallow | Extreme in-to-out | Feel more "across" the ball |
| Thin shots | No divot | Early extension or hanging back | Get weight forward, stay in posture |

## The Range Test

At the driving range, use the divot patterns to track progress:

1. Hit 10 balls with your natural swing
2. Note divot direction and depth for each
3. Work on your intended fix
4. Hit 10 more balls
5. Compare divot patterns

**If your divots change, your swing is changing.** It's immediate, free feedback.
