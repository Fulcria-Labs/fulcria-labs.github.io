A golf glove is your only physical connection to the club besides your grip. The right glove enhances feel, prevents slipping, and protects against blisters. The wrong one costs you control and comfort.

We have tested the leading golf gloves of 2026 to help you find your perfect fit.

## Why Golf Gloves Matter

**Grip Security**
A good glove provides consistent traction even when your hands sweat. Without one, humidity and perspiration create unpredictable grip pressure, leading to compensation swings.

**Feel and Control**
Premium leather gloves transmit feedback from the clubhead through your hands. This connection helps you sense impact quality and clubface position.

**Blister Prevention**
Repetitive swinging creates friction. A properly fitted glove distributes pressure and prevents the blisters that can end your range session early.

## Materials: Leather vs Synthetic vs Hybrid

### Cabretta Leather

The traditional choice used by tour professionals. Cabretta comes from the hair side of sheep or lambskin.

**Pros:**
- Superior feel and feedback
- Molds to your hand over time
- Natural breathability

**Cons:**
- Wears faster (10-15 rounds average)
- More expensive ($22-35)
- Less durable in wet conditions

### Synthetic Materials

Modern synthetics have closed the gap significantly. Microfiber and engineered materials offer durability without sacrificing too much feel.

**Pros:**
- Longer lasting (20-30+ rounds)
- More affordable ($12-20)
- Better wet weather performance
- Machine washable

**Cons:**
- Less feel than premium leather
- Can feel stiff when new
- May not breathe as well

### Hybrid Gloves

Combine leather palms with synthetic backs. The best of both worlds for most recreational golfers.

**Pros:**
- Good feel with better durability
- Strategic material placement
- Moderate price point ($18-28)

**Cons:**
- Seams between materials can irritate
- Neither best feel nor best durability

## Golf Glove Sizing Guide

### How to Measure

1. **Hand circumference:** Wrap measuring tape around your palm just below the knuckles, excluding thumb
2. **Finger length:** Measure from base of palm to tip of middle finger

### Size Chart

| Circumference | Finger Length | Mens Size | Womens Size |
|--------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| 7-7.5 inches | 7.25-7.5 inches | Small | - |
| 7.5-8 inches | 7.5-7.75 inches | Medium | Large |
| 8-8.5 inches | 7.75-8 inches | Medium-Large | - |
| 8.5-9 inches | 8-8.25 inches | Large | - |
| 9-9.5 inches | 8.25-8.5 inches | X-Large | - |
| 9.5-10 inches | 8.5+ inches | XX-Large | - |

### Fit Tips

- **Too tight:** Restricts movement, wears quickly, fingers bunch at tips
- **Too loose:** Wrinkles reduce feel, club slips in grip
- **Perfect fit:** Snug like a second skin with no bunching; fingers reach ends without excess

Most golfers err toward too loose. When trying gloves, make a fist - there should be minimal bunching across the palm.

## When to Replace Your Golf Glove

**Replace every 15-20 rounds** for leather gloves, or when you notice:

- **Holes developing:** Usually at the palm or finger joints first
- **Loss of tackiness:** The leather feels slick even when dry
- **Stretching:** The glove no longer fits snugly
- **Hardening:** Material becomes stiff and inflexible
- **Tearing at closure:** Velcro or tab area deteriorating

Rotating between 2-3 gloves extends the life of each by allowing moisture to fully dry between uses.

## Golf Glove Care Tips

**Storage**
- Lay flat or use a glove keeper to maintain shape
- Never crumple into pocket or bag
- Keep away from direct sunlight and heat

**During Rounds**
- Remove between shots to let hand breathe
- Avoid touching face or wiping sweat with gloved hand
- Keep backup glove in bag for wet conditions

**Cleaning**
- Leather: Wipe with damp cloth, air dry flat
- Synthetic: Hand wash with mild soap, air dry
- Never machine dry any golf glove

**Extend Lifespan**
- Rotate 2-3 gloves throughout a round
- Let gloves fully dry between uses (24+ hours)
- Store in cool, dry location

## FAQ

**Do I need to wear a golf glove?**
No rule requires one. But 95%+ of tour players wear gloves because the grip security and protection provide a meaningful advantage. Most amateurs benefit significantly.

**Why do golfers remove gloves for putting?**
Many prefer the direct feel of skin on grip for delicate putts. Putting requires less grip security than full swings. It is personal preference.

**Should I wear gloves on both hands?**
Standard is non-dominant hand only (left for right-handed golfers). Both hands reduces feel but some players prefer it in rain or extreme heat.

**How many gloves should I own?**
Minimum three: one for practice, one for rounds, one backup. Rotation extends lifespan significantly.
