Tempo is one of those things everyone talks about but few really understand. "Nice tempo" is a compliment. "Slow it down" is the most common advice for struggling golfers. But what is tempo, exactly, and how do you find yours?

## Tempo vs. Speed

First, let's clear up the confusion. Tempo is not how fast you swing. It's the ratio between your backswing and downswing.

Tour pros have been measured, and the most common tempo ratio is roughly 3:1. That means their backswing takes about three times longer than their downswing.

Here's the surprising part: this ratio is consistent whether a pro is swinging at 80% or 100%. The backswing might be faster on a hard swing, but so is the downswing—the ratio stays the same.

## Why Tempo Matters

Consistent tempo creates consistent results. When your timing varies, everything else varies too:
- Contact location on the face
- Club path
- Clubface angle at impact
- Sequence of body parts

Great ball strikers can repeat their tempo swing after swing. That's why Ernie Els looked so smooth hitting it 280 yards and Fred Couples seemed to barely swing yet the ball flew forever.

## Common Tempo Mistakes

**Too fast on the backswing:**
Many golfers snatch the club back quickly, thinking speed equals power. But a rushed backswing shortens your turn and throws off your transition.

**Pausing too long at the top:**
Some golfers overdo the "wait at the top" feeling and create a jerky, disconnected transition.

**Trying to muscle it from the top:**
The downswing should be a chain reaction, not a lunge. When you try to hit hard, you usually lose the sequence.

## How to Find Your Natural Tempo

### Method 1: The Counting Method

Say "one-two" on your backswing and "three" at impact. This creates approximately a 2:1 ratio—not quite tour tempo but a good starting point. As you get comfortable, lengthen the backswing count: "one-two" becomes "wuh-one-two."

### Method 2: The Song Method

Pick a song with a steady beat and match your swing to it. Many pros and teachers recommend specific songs—"Stayin' Alive" by the Bee Gees is a popular choice for beginners because the tempo is slow enough to prevent rushing.

### Method 3: The Rehearsal Method

Before every shot, make a smooth practice swing at 50% speed focusing only on rhythm. Then step up and replicate that feeling at full speed. The ratio should feel the same even though the swing is faster.

## Tempo Under Pressure

Here's where tempo really matters: on the first tee and on crucial shots. When you're nervous, your tempo almost always speeds up—specifically, the transition from backswing to downswing.

Build a pre-shot routine that includes a tempo reset. One slow, rhythmic practice swing before every real swing keeps your nervous system in check.

## The Connection to Power

Counterintuitively, better tempo often creates more distance. When you're rushed, your body segments fire out of order. When you're smooth, the kinetic chain works properly and energy transfers efficiently from ground to ball.

Many golfers have gained 10+ yards just by slowing their backswing down.

## How to Check Your Tempo

Film your swing and count frames. Most phone cameras shoot at 60 frames per second. Count frames from takeaway to top, then from top to impact. What's your ratio?
